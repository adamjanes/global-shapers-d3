About the Survey

The Global Shapers Annual Survey 2016 is designed with two lead questions:

  • How do young people see the world?
  • What do they want to do about it?

The study explores both perception and action in five dimensions across 4 distinct age-groups ranging from 18 to 35 years. The five dimensions are: Economy, Technology, Values, Governance and the Role of Business.

The survey received 26,615 total responses from 181 countries of which 20,079 were valid. Respondents include both members of the Global Shapers Community (2K) and young people who are not members of the Community. The survey is anonymous and does not track answers by personal identity. Responses were collected in cities by Survey Affiliates predominantly online, although in some cities responses were also collected offline. To reach young people without easy access to devices, workshops were often set up which provided devices that respondents could use to complete the survey. Interviews were discouraged to avoid any interpretation bias or unintended influence. No respondent was paid to participate in the survey.

The survey was available in 9 languages, including all UN languages.

The questions are designed to highlight perception as well as to provide action-oriented recommendations in five areas:

  • Economy and Global Outlook: Youth perspectives on how major socio-economic trends are reshaping the future of the global economy.
  • Technology and Innovation: How technology is transforming and redefining the modern life.
  • Governance: Governance, attitudes to governance and recommendations for related issues.
  • Values and Society: Explore how modern values are evolving or changing in the era of the fourth industrial revolution.
  • The Role of the Private Sector: Perspectives on the role of business in society and thoughts on jobs, organisations and related issues.

After the first part of the survey, there are 5 optional pathways that any respondent may choose to follow to provide additional insights. The pathways are an opportunity to go deeper in the areas listed above.

The four age groups are: 18-21; 22-26; 27-30; 31-35.

The Survey was designed by a Core Team at the headquarters of the Global Shapers Community and the World working with a team of over 40 Global Shapers from different countries and regions. In addition, consultation was conducted with various experts and stakeholders both within and outside the World Economic Forum’s communities. The mobilisation of responses was completed in close collaboration with members of the Global Shapers Community and also with the official Survey Affiliates.
